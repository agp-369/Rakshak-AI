# The Story of Rakshak AI — India's Disaster Guardian

## Core Narrative

**"Priya, an NCC cadet in Bihar, was trapped after a massive flood with no phone signal, no internet, and no way to call for help. She had one tool that worked: a phone running Rakshak AI."**

---

## The Character

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Name** | Priya Sharma |
| **Age** | 19 |
| **Background** | NCC cadet, 2nd year B.Sc. Nursing student from Muzaffarpur, Bihar |
| **Role** | First responder at a local level — trained in basic life support, not a doctor |
| **Dream** | To serve rural India where healthcare is scarce |

---

## The Disaster (Bihar Floods 2026 — Fictional but Plausible)

- **Location**: Muzaffarpur district, Bihar
- **Event**: Sudden embankment breach after 3 days of continuous monsoon rain
- **Affected**: 50+ villages submerged, thousands displaced
- **Challenge**: No cellular network, roads washed away, nearest hospital 40 km
- **Priya's situation**: She's at a temporary relief camp with 200+ survivors, no doctor, limited supplies, no connectivity

---

## Emotional Arc

### Act 1: Before Disaster
Priya is a normal college student. She downloads Rakshak AI on her ₹12,000 Samsung Galaxy M05 after her brother's friend — a techie in Patna — shows her the app. "Just in case," he says. She never thinks she'll use it.

### Act 2: The Flood
*Scene*: 3 AM. Water enters the village. Priya's family escapes to higher ground. At the relief camp, people are injured — a child with deep cuts, an elderly man struggling to breathe, a pregnant woman in distress. No doctor. No network. Panic.

Priya remembers the app. She opens Rakshak AI.

### Act 3: Using Rakshak AI
- She describes a patient in Hindi: *"सांस नहीं ले रहा, नब्ज़ कमज़ोर"* 
- Gemma 4 on-device analyzes: RED — Immediate. Start rescue breathing.
- She triages 47 patients in 2 hours using voice descriptions
- QR mesh sync lets nearby volunteers access her triage data offline
- The app's SOS beacon guides an NDRF boat to the camp location

### Act 4: Aftermath
- All critically injured patients evacuated by NDRF within 4 hours of triage
- Zero preventable deaths at Priya's camp
- The District Magistrate commends the use of "offline AI triage"
- Priya becomes a local hero — and advocates for Rakshak AI deployment across Bihar's flood-prone districts
